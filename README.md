# Classical and Bayesian Estimations of Performance Measures in a Single-Server Markovian Queueing System

This repository contains the implementation and computational experiments related to the article:

**S. K. Singh, F. R. B. Cruz, E. S. Gomes, A. D. Banik**  
*Classical and Bayesian estimations of performance measures in a single-server Markovian queueing system based on arrivals during service times*  
Communications in Statistics - Theory and Methods, 2024  

DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/03610926.2022.2155789

## Abstract

This repository provides the computational implementation supporting the numerical experiments presented in the article on classical and Bayesian estimation of performance measures in single-server Markovian queueing systems.

The study considers a single-server Markovian queueing system based on observations of the number of customer arrivals during the service time of a customer. The traffic intensity is estimated together with key performance measures, including the average queue length and the expected number of customers in the system.

Both classical and Bayesian inferential frameworks are developed. In the Bayesian setting, three prior distributions for the traffic intensity parameter are considered under two different loss functions. The predictive distribution of the number of customer arrivals during service time and equal-tailed credible regions for the traffic intensity are also obtained.

The proposed methodologies are illustrated through numerical examples based on simulation studies.

## Repository Structure

- `experiments.R`  
  Contains the code required to reproduce all numerical experiments presented in the article.

- `plots.R`  
  Reproduces all figures presented in the article.

- `results/`  
  Stores numerical outputs generated by the experiments.

- `figures/`  
  Stores generated plots and graphical outputs.

## Reproducibility

To reproduce the results reported in the paper:

1. Run:

```r
source("experiments.R")
```
