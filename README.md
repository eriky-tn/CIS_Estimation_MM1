# Classical and Bayesian Estimations of Performance Measures in a Single-Server Markovian Queueing System based on arrivals during service times

This repository contains the implementation and computational experiments related to the article:

**S. K. Singh, F. R. B. Cruz, E. S. Gomes, A. D. Banik**  
*Classical and Bayesian estimations of performance measures in a single-server Markovian queueing system based on arrivals during service times*  
Communications in Statistics - Theory and Methods, 2024  

DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/03610926.2022.2155789

## Abstract

The present study considers a single-server Markovian queueing sys-tem by observing the number of customer arrivals during the servicetime of a customer. We estimate the traffic intensity in this queueingsystem along with the average queue length and the expected num-ber of customers in the system. We propose classical and Bayesianframeworks to estimate the parameters of interest. In the Bayesiansetup, three forms of prior distributions for q and two loss functionsare considered. Furthermore, the predictive distribution of the num-ber of customer arrivals during the service time of a customer andthe equal-tailed credible region of q are obtained. The aforemen-tioned approaches are illustrated with numerical examples based onsimulation studies.

## Repository Structure

- `experiments.R`  
  Contains the code required to reproduce all numerical experiments presented in the article.

- `results/`  
  Stores numerical outputs generated by the experiments.

- `figures/`  
  Stores generated plots and graphical outputs.

## Reproducibility

To reproduce the results reported in the paper:

1. Run:
